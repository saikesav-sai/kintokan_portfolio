Diploma in Radiology

About

A diploma in Radiology equips students with knowledge of medical imaging techniques, radiographic procedures, and patient care. It prepares individuals for careers in diagnostic radiology and further studies in medical imaging.

Key Components

  • Medical Imaging Techniques: X-rays, MRI, CT scans, and ultrasound procedures.
  • Radiographic Procedures: Proper imaging techniques, positioning, and safety protocols.
  • Anatomy and Physiology: Understanding human anatomy for accurate imaging interpretation.
  • Radiation Safety: Exposure control, protective measures, and regulatory compliance.
  • Patient Care: Communication skills, patient preparation, and ethical considerations.

Practical Training

  • Internships in hospitals, diagnostic centers, or radiology departments.
  • Hands-on training with imaging equipment and radiographic procedures.
  • Case studies and simulations to develop diagnostic skills.
