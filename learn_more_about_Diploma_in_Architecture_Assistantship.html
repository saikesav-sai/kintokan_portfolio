Diploma in Architecture Assistantship

Comprehensive training in architectural design and drafting

About

A diploma in Architecture Assistantship equips students with the fundamental knowledge and technical skills required to assist architects in designing, planning, and managing building projects. The program covers architectural drafting, construction techniques, and computer-aided design (CAD).

Key Components

  • Architectural Drawing & Drafting: Learning to create technical drawings, floor plans, and elevations.
  • Building Materials & Construction: Understanding different materials and construction methodologies.
  • Computer-Aided Design (CAD): Training in AutoCAD, Revit, and other design software.
  • Structural Engineering Basics: Introduction to structural concepts in buildings.
  • Estimating & Costing: Learning how to estimate project costs and prepare budgets.

Practical Training

  • Hands-on experience with CAD software and architectural drafting tools.
  • Site visits to understand real-world construction practices.
  • Internships with architecture firms and construction companies.
  • Portfolio development to showcase architectural designs.
