Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology

About

A diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (MLT) trains students in laboratory testing, diagnostic procedures, and medical research. The program equips learners with practical skills in analyzing body fluids, identifying diseases, and handling laboratory equipment.

Key Components

  • Clinical Biochemistry: Understanding chemical processes within the human body.
  • Hematology: Study of blood, blood disorders, and diagnostic techniques.
  • Microbiology: Identification and analysis of microorganisms causing diseases.
  • Pathology: Examination of tissues, organs, and bodily fluids for disease detection.
  • Medical Equipment Handling: Training in using laboratory instruments and diagnostic tools.

Practical Training

  • Internships in hospitals, diagnostic centers, and research laboratories.
  • Hands-on experience with blood tests, microscopy, and biochemical analysis.
  • Training in laboratory safety, quality control, and report interpretation.

Career Opportunities

  • Medical Laboratory Technician
  • Pathology Lab Assistant
  • Clinical Research Associate
  • Diagnostic Lab Technologist
  • Blood Bank Technician
