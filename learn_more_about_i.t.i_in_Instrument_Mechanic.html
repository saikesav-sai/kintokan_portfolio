ITI in Instrument Mechanic

Hands-on training in instrumentation, maintenance, and calibration

About

The ITI in Instrument Mechanic program provides technical education in the installation, maintenance, and repair of measuring and control instruments used in various industries, including manufacturing, power plants, and refineries.

Key Components

  • Instrumentation Basics: Principles of measurement and control systems.
  • Electrical & Electronic Components: Study of sensors, transducers, and circuits.
  • Maintenance & Calibration: Techniques for maintaining industrial instruments.
  • Automation & PLC: Introduction to Programmable Logic Controllers and automation systems.
  • Safety & Standards: Industrial safety protocols and quality control measures.

Practical Training

  • Hands-on experience with calibration tools and measurement devices.
  • Industrial visits and real-time troubleshooting workshops.
  • Internships in manufacturing plants, power stations, and refineries.

Career Opportunities

  • Instrumentation Technician
  • Maintenance Engineer
  • Automation Technician
  • Quality Control Inspector
  • Industrial Equipment Technician

Further Studies

  • Diploma or Bachelor's degree in Instrumentation Engineering.
  • Certifications in PLC programming, industrial automation, and safety standards.

Summary

The ITI in Instrument Mechanic program equips students with technical expertise in instrumentation and automation, preparing them for careers in industrial maintenance, control systems, and process industries.
